26 /**
27  * @brief The functions in this namespace contain the expressions for the various terms of the configurational force (https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevB.97.165132)
28  * for both periodic (see Eq. 38) and non-periodic (see Eqs. 28-29) case.
29  *
30  * Basically, the configurational force is the Gateaux derivative
31  * of the Kohn-Sham saddle point problem with respect to perturbations of the underlying space due to generic generator (which can be affine perturbation in
32  * case of stress computation or an atom centered generator with a compact support for computing the forces). The terms in the configurational force can be
33  * grouped into two types: one type can be written as contraction of Eshelby tensors (second order tensor) with the gradient of the Generator. Another type involves
34  * contraction of first order tensors with the Generator. The functions in this class provide expressions for the left side of the contraction operation- the second
35  * order Eshelby tensors (denoted by E*) and the first order force tensors (denoted by F*).
36  * General nomenclature of the input arguments:
37  * a) phiTot- total electrostatic potential
38  * b) phiExt- sum of electrostatic potential from all nuclear charges
39  * c) rho- electron density
40  * d) gradRho- gradient of electron density
41  * e) exc- exchange correlation energy
42  * f) derExcGradRho- derivative of exc with gradient of rho
43  * g) psiBegin- begin iterator to vector eigenvectors stored as a flattened array over k points and number of eigenvectors for each k point
44  * (periodic case has complex valued eigenvectors which is why Tensor<1,2,VectorizedArray<double> is used in functions for periodic case)
45  * h) gradPsiBegin- gradient of eigenvectors
46  * i) eigenValues- Kohn sham grounstate eigenvalues stored in a vector. For periodic problems with multiple k points the outer vector should be over k points
47  * j) tVal- smearing temperature in K
48  * k) pseudoVLoc- local part of the pseuodopotential
49  * l) gradPseudoVLoc- gradient of local part of pseudopotential
50  * m) ZetaDeltaV- nonlocal pseudowavefunctions times deltaV (see Eq. 11 in https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevB.97.165132)
51  * n) gradZetaDeltaV- gradient of ZetaDeltaV
52  * o) projectorKetTimesPsiTimesV- nonlocal pseudopotential projector ket times eigenvectors which are precomputed.
53  * The nonlocal pseudopotential constants are also multiplied to this quantity. (see Eq. 11 in https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevB.97.165132)
54  *
55  * @author Sambit Das
56  */
